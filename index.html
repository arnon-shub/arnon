Not everyone you talk to belongs in your contacts. A stranger, a one-time conversation, someone you'd rather not give your number to — talk, then close the tab. Nothing to delete, because nothing was saved.

Features

End-to-end encrypted

ECDH key exchange with AES-256-GCM. Every conversation generates fresh keys. A safety number lets both sides confirm no one is in the middle. Runs entirely in your browser via Web Crypto API.

No trace

Close the tab and everything is destroyed. Keys, messages, identity — all gone. Nothing saved anywhere.

No account

No phone number. No email. No registration. No identity. You're nobody — by design.

No download

Works in any browser. Phone, laptop, tablet. Nothing to install. Send a link, start chatting.

Text & voice

Send text messages and voice notes up to 30 seconds. Everything encrypted end-to-end. Nothing stored. No photos, videos, or file sharing — by design.

Self-destruct

Set a timer when you create a conversation. When time's up, the room is destroyed on both sides — even if nobody closes the tab.

Small enough to verify

The whole thing — app and relay combined — is about 600 lines. Small enough to read every line yourself in an afternoon, instead of trusting that someone audited a codebase you'll never open. All open source on GitHub, and the crypto runs in your browser.

Blind relay

Messages pass through a relay that holds only encrypted blobs in memory — nothing is written to disk. No accounts, no stored logs. Hosted in Helsinki, EU (GDPR).

Other private messengers store your account and history on a device or a server. Arnon runs entirely in your browser — no install, no sign-up, and nothing kept after you close the tab.

Arnon is a free, self-hostable side project from Particular Ltd — not a funded startup, just a single relay server and a browser app designed to disappear.

What Arnon protects — and what it doesn't

Arnon is end-to-end encrypted and keeps nothing after you close the tab — but because the app loads fresh in your browser each time, you trust whoever serves it. Against an adversary who can compromise the server, DNS, or TLS to target you specifically, an installed, independently verified client like Signal or SimpleX is stronger; Arnon is built for low-friction, identity-free privacy, not for surviving a targeted server compromise.
